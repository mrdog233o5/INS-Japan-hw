Japan Holiday Guide

Mindful Travel Tips

1. Respect for Customs: While Japan is changing, it's important to respect local customs and traditions. Learn a few basic Japanese phrases, bow when greeting, and adhere to proper behaviour when visiting temples and shrines.
2. Be respectful to the emperor: Japan people values their emperor a lot, and there are new laws to make people respect their emperor, so make sure that you respect the emperor when visiting Japan.
3. Cultural Sensitivity: Be mindful of the cultural nuances and sensitivities. For example, avoid pointing with your finger, as it is considered impolite. Instead, use an open hand or gesture with your chin. And, remember to remove your shoes when entering traditional Japanese houses or certain establishments.

Things To See

1. Tokyo: Go to core of Japan, the nation's capital, Tokyo, and you will be able to see the Imperial Palace, the symbol of Japan's imperial rule. Walk in the bustling streets, where traditional shops stand with modern shops, reflecting the blend of old and new.
2. Kyoto: Experience the ancient temples and shrines, this is the best place to see Japan's traditional culture. Don't miss the of Kinkaku-ji, the Golden Pavilion, and the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove.

Changes To Look For

1. Political: You can see Japan changing from a feudal system to a centralized government led by the Emperor. Be prepared to see the influence of the Meiji government.
2. Social Modernization: You will notice that Japan will use more Western clothes and fashion in the city, and mixing with some traditional Japanese clothes. It's an good time to see japan changing from its traditional culture to more Western culture.
3. Economic Revolution: Look for the growing cities, new industries, and better economy. It's a great time to see Japan's economic growth and the Western technologies.
